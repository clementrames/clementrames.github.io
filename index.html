Passionate researcher exploring the energy-mobility nexus from a data-driven, human-centered perspective at the National Renewable Energy Lab. Innovating to accelerate the transition to a sustainable and equitable mobility ecosystem; striving to influence human behavior to create more livable cities. Currently investigating the synergies between transportation electrification, sharing, and automation through DOE’s SMART Mobility consortium, in collaboration with five National Labs. Fascinated by bio-inspired design, from swarm robotics to neural networks, I helped develop a MEMS piezoelectric microphone emulating a fly’s hearing organ. A lifelong learner, design thinker and avid traveler, I’ve lived and worked in multiple countries across Europe and America and thrive in multicultural environments. In the coming years, I hope to keep bridging disciplines to try and foresee how new technologies will transform our energy, mobility, water, information and public health systems, and be mindful of these impacts when designing solutions for the future.
